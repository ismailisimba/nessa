About Us

Founded in 2019, Nessa Foundation is a Non-Governmental Organization registered under the Ministry of Health, Community Development, Gender, Elderly and Children-Community Development. Starting with a team of five, Nessa Foundation aims to support and promote the following.

Objectives

  • To promote communities through socio-economic issues like education, healthcare, nutrition, water, environment, and sanitation.
  • To empower vulnerable groups to participate in income-generating activities for socio-economic transformations of their homesteads.
  • To empower youth in socio-economic issues and leadership.
  • To enhance preventive measures against HIV/AIDS and promote initiatives among communities caring for individuals and families affected by AIDS.
  • To enhance women's and children's inclusion in health and nutrition issues.
  • To offer guidance and services to persons including children, youths, and adults.

Mission

Our mission is to achieve transformation through self-help initiatives and relief assistance, giving all necessary support to communities in need and vulnerable groups.

Vision

We seek a world of hope that the lives of our communities become safe, healthy, independent, and productive.

Mount Kilimanjaro Reforestation

Mount Kilimanjaro, enshrined in our collective memory through its iconic cap of glaciers and the fact that it's the highest mountain in Africa and the highest free-standing mountain in the world, could soon change drastically. Scientific research estimates that its ice could be gone entirely by 2040, leading to a reduction in tourism, water shortages and food shortages.

Nessa is working with the communities in districts surrounding the mountain to plant trees to restore the environment around the mountain.

Postpartum Mental Health

Childbirth is a major physical and emotional moment for the mother. It can act as a social stressor in a woman’s life, causing mental health problems such as postpartum depression (PPD) and postpartum psychosis (PPP). PPD and PPP may occur during the early days after childbirth. In most women, they will manifest as some mental disturbance like mood swings and mild depression (also known as baby blues), but in extreme cases, PTSD (Post Traumatic Stress Disorder) or full-blown psychosis may occur.

Nessa aims to raise awareness and educate women, health workers, and the community. Starting in Dar es Salaam, Morogoro, and Pwani, we are going to work with health professional to access the current situation and introduce programs to help mothers and families.
